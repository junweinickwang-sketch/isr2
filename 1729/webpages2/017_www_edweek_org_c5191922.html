A Comprehensive List of States with Cellphone Bans or Restrictions in Schools

An examination of the laws and policies concerning cellphone use in schools across the states.

By Arianna Prothero, Lauraine Langreo & Alyson Klein â€” June 28, 2024

The introduction of the iPhone on June 29, 2007, marked the beginning of the modern smartphone era, a period into which almost every current K-12 student was born.

Nevertheless, students' access to personal electronic devices during school hours is facing the possibility of significant reduction. A rising number of education officials and lawmakers are instituting new cellphone regulations, with discussions now firmly leaning towards the implementation of stricter controls. These advocates contend that restricting device access will yield positive outcomes for students' mental well-being and academic achievement.

An Education Week analysis reveals that a minimum of 31 states, along with the District of Columbia, now mandate that school districts prohibit or limit student cellphone usage. Additionally, several other states are encouraging or suggesting that local districts establish their own prohibitions or restrictive measures.

While the conversation about cellphones in educational settings evolves, Education Week is actively monitoring state legislation and policies designed to restrict student access to these devices during school hours.
