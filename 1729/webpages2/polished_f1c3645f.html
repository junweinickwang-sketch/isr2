Cellphones in the Classroom: Should It Be Allowed?

Dr. Benjamin Washington
Project Director for 21st Century Community Learning Centers; Ed.D. Educational Leadership
Request Info

Home Educators Blog

Today’s classrooms have access to numerous forms of educational technology. Examples of these technology tools include laptops, desktops, Chromebooks, SMARTBoards, and even cell phones. However, giving students access to cell phones is controversial in terms of student engagement.

For engaging teachers with experience in classroom management, this can be a net positive. However, whether the teacher is a veteran or a new educator, students using cell phones in the classroom can also be challenging regarding teaching and learning.

Pros of Cellphones in the Classroom

For some parents and educators, there are pros to having cellphones in the classroom. Some proactive arguments for cellphone use include accelerated learning, access to digital content, and a communication tool to parents for student safety.

Safety

Having cellphones accessible in the classroom offers a real sense of security. In the event of an emergency, whether it’s a medical issue or something more serious, students can quickly reach out for help. Plus, it opens up a direct line of communication with parents.

If a student isn’t feeling well or there’s an unexpected change in the day, they can easily let their parents know, and educators can also get in touch with families quickly if needed. That peace of mind, knowing we have these immediate communication channels, is a significant benefit.

Educational Games

Oftentimes, teachers can also facilitate educational games such as Kahoot, Wordle, and Minecraft Education. 10 Best Educational Mobile Games, is an article that details mobile games students can use to enhance learning both in the classroom and at home.

Cellphone Lessons

In the hands of a capable teacher, cell phones can be used to facilitate group activities. For example, for educators focused on STEM activities, a cell phone can even be explored in a lesson. Science teachers, business education teachers, and students who are interested in information technology (IT) may find researching the inner components of a cell phone interesting.

STEM teachers and science teachers have a multitude of ways to incorporate learning with cell phones and digital devices. Learning what’s inside of a cell phone can be a way for students to learn about the technology they use every day, and how it can be beneficial for communication and networking.

Integrating cellphones thoughtfully can be a real win when it comes to prepping our students for the real world. Think about it – they’re already living in a digital landscape, right? So, instead of fighting it, we can use those devices to actively teach them how to navigate it responsibly.

We can analyze online sources together, discuss what makes information credible (or not!), and practice ethical online communication. It’s about guiding them to be informed, discerning digital citizens, and their own phones are the perfect tools to make that learning tangible and relevant.

In order to understand the pros of cellphone usage in the classroom it is also important to look into what the cons of cellphones in the classroom are.

Cons of Cellphones in the Classroom

Distractions, Cyberbulling, Addiction

With the pros of cellphone usage in schools, there are also cons, including distractions, lack of focus, and even long-term emotional and mental health issues. This can be especially true for students who engage in social media daily.  One of the biggest cons of having cellphones in the classroom is the distraction it gives students during quality instructional time.

Distraction is certainly a major concern, as notifications and the allure of social media can easily pull a student’s attention away from lessons. This not only impacts the individual student’s learning but can also disrupt the entire classroom environment.

Additionally, with the rise of group chats and the ability to connect multiple students all at once, this can quickly turn into a tool for cyberbullying. This pervasive connectivity means that incidents can escalate rapidly and extend beyond school hours, making it difficult for educators and parents to intervene effectively. The constant potential for harassment through these devices can create a hostile learning environment, impacting students’ mental well-being and academic performance.

Many students need to realize the negative effects, such as cellphone addiction, that the overuse of cell phones can bring them. AsapSCIENCE has created a video entitled How Your Phone is Changing You. This interactive video provides a detailed overview of how using a cell phone constantly can literally change brain function and the ability to think in a higher cognitive manner.

Many schools are finding ways to manage cell phone use in schools, while others have banned cell phones in schools altogether. Many schools do not allow cell phones in the elementary setting but may give middle school students more access. Meanwhile, other schools may restrict cell phones only in the classroom or completely.

The Los Angeles Unified School District (LAUSD) will institute a ban on cell phones in all of its schools. As the United States’ second-largest school district with over 427,795 students, this is an ambitious move. The cell phone ban will begin in 2025.

There are a variety of ways that teachers and parents can guide healthy smartphone habits. This includes choosing specific educational content for students, designating device-free time in classrooms and at home, and modeling proper expectations and behavior for phone use.

Managing cell phones in schools should be a priority to protect the learning environment for students. Choosing if a school should accept cellphones in the learning environment needs to be a consensus with the school building and school district policies.

Should Cellphones Be Allowed in the Classroom?

Comparing the pros and cons of cell phones being used in classrooms is very important. With the explosion of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in education, business, and entertainment it is vital to see where there are positive and negative uses of technology.

There are different AI content detection tools for teachers to use. Decisions about whether cell phones should be allowed in schools can be made between the school administration and teachers.

Additionally, there needs to be specific policies put in place and communicated to parents so that school officials and parents are on the same page for cellphone use or the lack thereof.

It can be beneficial if there is engaging facilitation of learning with well-planned lessons and parameters for cellphone use. However, poor classroom management and a lack of specific rules and routines for cellphone use can be disastrous to student learning outcomes.

As the use of technology continues to grow in education, understanding a balance between project-based learning, direct instruction, and the use of educational technology tools will continue to be important.
