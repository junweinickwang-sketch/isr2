What are some alternative strategies to prohibiting smartphones in educational institutions that can effectively mitigate their potential negative consequences?

Answer by Russ Ogman
Completed studies for a Bachelor of Science in Biology & Medical Laboratory Technology at Florida International University in 1995.

One potential solution involves permitting smartphones on school grounds while students are prohibited from being present.

An alternative approach is to permit both students and smartphones in schools, with the stipulation that the devices must not have batteries.

Another strategy involves allowing students and smartphones on campus, but implementing restrictions that limit phone functionality to parental contact only.

A different method involves downgrading smartphones to basic feature phones while simultaneously elevating the academic engagement of students.

One could also arrange seating such that each male student sits beside a female student, and vice versa, which would likely serve as a significant distraction from their mobile devices.

It is also possible to integrate artificial intelligence into each smartphone, programming it to verbally prompt the student to disengage from the device and refocus on the classroom lesson during instructional time.
